What Makes an AI Audit Different?

Traditional audits often inspect stable systems, repeatable processes, and evidence trails that already have mature control language. AI audits add a moving target: statistical behavior, training data, model updates, deployment context, and claims that can fail outside the test set.

The core question is still familiar: what is being claimed, what evidence supports it, who owns the risk, and what controls reduce it to an acceptable level?

A simple audit frame

  • Inventory the AI system, its owners, its users, and its business purpose.
  • Identify consequential claims about performance, safety, fairness, security, and compliance.
  • Trace each claim to tests, documentation, monitoring, and accountable signoff.
  • Assess whether controls remain valid after model, data, policy, or vendor changes.
