Fairness

Bias and Fairness

What to inspect

  • Groups likely to be affected, including proxy variables and missing populations.
  • Allocation harms, quality-of-service harms, stereotyping, erasure, and feedback loops.
  • Metric selection and the tradeoffs hidden by a single fairness score.
  • Complaint, appeal, override, and remediation mechanisms.

Evidence standard

Fairness evidence should show who was considered, which harms were tested, why the metrics fit the use case, and how unresolved gaps are owned.
